About Me

My Story

I'm Abraham Olenslager, a Pre-Business student at Brigham Young University with a deep passion for entrepreneurship, public advocacy, and creating positive change through business. From founding BOOST Candy Co. to leading political campaign recruitment efforts, I thrive on building things from the ground up and connecting with people.

My journey has taken me from the debate stage—where I earned state championships and national recognition—to the streets of Buenos Aires, Argentina, where I served a two-year mission and became fluent in Spanish. These experiences taught me the value of clear communication, cultural understanding, and the power of persuasive storytelling.

What Drives Me

I believe in the intersection of business and civic engagement. Whether I'm lobbying legislators at the Utah State Capitol, training sales teams, or developing a new product, I'm motivated by the opportunity to make a tangible impact. I see entrepreneurship not just as building companies, but as solving real problems and creating value for communities.

Beyond business, I'm committed to free market principles and civic advocacy. As Outreach Coordinator for Turning Point USA at BYU, I work to engage students in conversations about policy, economics, and the role of entrepreneurship in society.
